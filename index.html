Transmission-based Precautions

Methicillin resistant Staphylococcus aureus (MRSA).


Suspected or known disease or microorganism

Methicillin resistant Staphylococcus aureus (MRSA).

Clinical presentation

  • Asymptomatic or various infections of skin, soft tissue, pneumonia, bacteraemia, urinary tract, etc.

Infectious substances

  • Surface skin, infected or colonised secretions, excretions

Duration of precautions

  • Contact IPC prior to stopping precautions. Generally three negative screens if nasal or groin colonised. If patient has wound, drain, sputa positive with MRSA, patient may require contact precautions for extended period.

Period of Communicability

  • Variable

Incubation Period

  • Variable

Disinfection

Use hypochlorite 1,000ppm for daily routine and terminal disinfection of devices, surfaces, equipment and the environment.
